DeepSeek, AI Applications & Huawei's PanGu

Real-world AI in action — and the debates around it. This lesson finishes Chapter 01.

1. DeepSeek — Why It Matters

DeepSeek is a Chinese AI company (founded 2023, part of the hedge fund High-Flyer) that released a series of large language models — most notably DeepSeek-R1 — that stunned the AI world in early 2025.

Why the shock? Because DeepSeek achieved performance comparable to top US models (like GPT-4) at a fraction of the cost — reportedly trained for under $6 million, while comparable US models cost hundreds of millions.

What Makes DeepSeek Special
  • Efficiency: Used a Mixture of Experts (MoE) architecture — only activates a small portion of the model per query, saving compute
  • Reinforcement Learning: Used RL to teach the model to reason step-by-step without expensive human labelling
  • Open weights: Released the model weights publicly, enabling anyone to run it locally
  • Cost: Dramatically cheaper to train than equivalent Western models
Influence on AI Development
  • Proved that efficiency matters as much as raw compute — the "bigger is always better" assumption was challenged
  • Accelerated open-source AI — more developers could now run capable models without expensive cloud services
  • Increased global competition in AI, pushing US labs to reconsider their approaches
  • Demonstrated that non-US companies could reach the AI frontier with smart engineering
⚠ Exam Alert
Know: DeepSeek is significant because it achieved frontier performance at dramatically lower cost using efficiency innovations (MoE + RL), not just brute-force compute. It influenced future AI by showing efficiency is a viable alternative to scale.

2. AI Application History — From Perceiving to Creating

AI applications have evolved through distinct eras. Understanding this arc helps you see where we are now and why Foundation Models are such a big deal:

Era 1
Perception — AI learns to sense the world
Image recognition, speech-to-text, object detection. AI can see and hear.
Era 2
Understanding — AI learns to interpret meaning
Sentiment analysis, translation, question answering. AI can read and understand.
Era 3 ★
Creation & Generation — AI creates new things
ChatGPT, DALL-E, code generation, music. AI can write, paint, and code. We are here.

ChatGPT (launched Nov 2022) was the inflection point — the first AI product to demonstrate generation at scale to a mass audience. It reached 100 million users in 2 months, faster than any product in history. Other systems followed: Gemini, Claude, Copilot, Mistral, Qwen, and more.

⚠ Exam Alert
Know the three phases: Perception → Understanding → Creation/Generation. Know that ChatGPT was the landmark application that made Generation-era AI mainstream.

3. Real-World AI Application Cases

The exam may test your awareness of how AI is applied in specific industries. Two cases worth knowing:

⛏ Intelligent Mining
AI is used in mining to monitor equipment health, predict failures before they happen, detect hazards, and optimize excavation routes. This reduces accidents and increases efficiency in environments too dangerous for constant human presence.
🦉 AI Helps Protect Nature (Shield of Sound — Greece)
Microphones placed in nature parks record audio. AI models identify animal calls — including endangered species and poachers' equipment — in real time. Rangers are alerted to illegal activity and rare animal sightings without needing to patrol vast areas constantly.

More broadly, AI powers AI-Powered Production — factories where AI monitors production lines, detects defects automatically, and adjusts processes in real time without human intervention.

4. AI for Industries: Huawei's PanGu Model

This section is heavily exam-weighted for Huawei certification. PanGu is Huawei's family of Foundation Models designed specifically for industry use.

The Three-Layer Foundation Model Hierarchy

PanGu introduced a three-tier structure that shows how a general model can be specialized step-by-step for real industry use:

Basic → Industry → Scenario: each layer is more specialized

① Basic Model — PanGu Natural Language Model
A massive general-purpose LLM trained on broad data. It has no industry specialization yet — it just has strong language understanding and generation ability. Think of it as the "raw intelligence."
② Industry Model — PanGu Government Model
The basic model is fine-tuned on government-specific data: laws, regulations, official documents, administrative processes. Now it understands government workflows, terminology, and compliance requirements. But it's still quite general within that industry.
③ Scenario Model — Smart Government Chatbot
The industry model is further tuned for a specific task: answering citizens' questions about government services. It knows exactly what documents you need to apply for a permit, what the processing time is, where to go — highly specific, highly practical.
⚠ Exam Alert
Memorize the three tiers: Basic Model (LLM) → Industry Model (Dialog) → Scenario Model (Intelligent Chatbot). Know the government example: PanGu NL Model → PanGu Government Model → Smart Government Chatbot. The direction is always: general → specialized.

PanGu models are deployed across many sectors: industrial manufacturing, device inspection, process prediction, smart cities, marketing, legal knowledge, meeting assistance, film production, and public sector services.

5. Huawei's AI Solutions: Ascend & ModelArts

PanGu runs on Huawei's AI infrastructure. Know these four components:

🔲 Ascend AI Processors
Huawei's custom AI chips — the hardware equivalent of NVIDIA's GPUs, but designed specifically for AI workloads. They enable large-scale intelligent computing. The Ascend series is Huawei's answer to GPU dependence.
🏗 Ascend AI Basic Software and Hardware Platform
The full stack of hardware + software (drivers, compilers, runtime) that makes the Ascend chips useful. Software alone or hardware alone is not enough — you need the full platform.
🚀 Ascend AI Enables Innovative Foundation Models
The Ascend platform is what powers PanGu and other large Huawei foundation models at scale. It's the "engine room" for Huawei AI.
🛠 ModelArts
Huawei's one-stop AI development platform. It covers the full AI lifecycle: data labelling → model training → model deployment. It's built on top of Ascend chips and is designed for both beginners and experts. Think of it as Huawei's equivalent of AWS SageMaker or Google Vertex AI.
⚠ Exam Alert
Know: Ascend = AI chips (hardware). ModelArts = full-lifecycle AI development platform (software). ModelArts uses Ascend chips under the hood. This stack is Huawei's proprietary alternative to GPU-based cloud AI.

6. Debates and the Future of AI

The exam may ask about the ethical debates and future directions of AI. Know these key tensions:

👁 Is Seeing Still Believing?
AI can generate realistic fake photos, videos (deepfakes), and audio. This breaks the assumption that visual evidence is trustworthy. Courts, journalism, and social media all face new challenges in verifying authenticity.
⚖️ Ethical Issues in AI
AI systems can amplify human bias (training data reflects historical inequalities), make life-affecting decisions (loan approval, parole, hiring) without human review, and lack accountability when things go wrong.
💼 Will You Lose Your Job?
AI automates routine cognitive tasks (data entry, basic analysis, code scaffolding). Jobs most at risk: repetitive, rule-based roles. Jobs least at risk (for now): those requiring physical dexterity, deep human relationships, and novel creative thinking. The consensus: AI changes jobs more than it eliminates them — but transitions can be painful.
🔒 Privacy & Data Security
AI models are trained on vast amounts of data — some of which may have been collected without consent. Models can also memorize and leak private information. Regulations like GDPR attempt to address this.

The Future of AI

📈 Value Dimension
Wide application across industries, growing AI market, higher production efficiency. AI is projected to add trillions in economic value globally over the coming decade.
🧬 Technology: AGI
Artificial General Intelligence — AI that can do anything a human can. The long-term moonshot. No timeline consensus; some researchers say decades away, some say never in current form.
🤖 Technology: Embodied AI
AI that has a physical body and interacts with the physical world — robots that can see, move, and manipulate objects. This combines CV, NLP, motor control, and real-time decision-making. Examples: humanoid robots (Figure, Optimus), warehouse robots.
📱 AI Devices
AI running on end-user devices (phones, laptops, AR glasses) rather than in the cloud. Enables offline AI, lower latency, and better privacy. Huawei's Mate series and Apple's on-device AI features are examples.
⚠ Exam Alert
Know the two future technology directions: AGI (general-purpose intelligence, doesn't exist yet) and Embodied AI (AI with a physical body that acts in the real world). These are distinct from each other.
